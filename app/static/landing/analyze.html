Analyze

Researchers are seeking a modern architecture for large-scale collaborative text analysis. Large text data collections are already collected and coded for basic and applied research in many fields. Yet the manual annotation of the text—the coding of various corpora—is often conducted in an ad hoc, inconsistent, nonreplicable, and unreliable manner. Further, coding is expensive, laborious, challenging, and too often idiosyncratic (MacQueen et al., 1999; Madero et al., 2006). SOCRATES will start addressing these issues, providing a platform for rapid and easy qualitative annotation and support for sharing and replication of work.

The goals of developing and attaining widespread acceptance of Internet-enabled data-sharing protocols (King, 2007), as well as establishing free, open source platforms for coding text and for sharing and replicating results were outlined in the research community as early as the 1990s (Gleditsch & Metelits, 2003). This work is now well advanced for quantitative data sharing (Altman & King, 2006), but qualitative data sharing lags behind in such standards. A top priority of this project is to develop a set of standards for text data research applications and to implement the standards consistently among a wide-reaching community of researchers.
